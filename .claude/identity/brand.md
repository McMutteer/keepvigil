# Vigil — Brand Guide

## The Story

Vigil's logo is "El Ángulo" — a V formed by two lines converging to a single amber point. The V carries a double reading: it's the first letter of "Vigil" AND a check mark (✓) seen from above. Vigilance + Verification in one stroke.

The amber point where the lines meet is the moment of truth — where analysis becomes score. It's warm against the cold precision of the lines, representing the human intent behind the machine: a developer who needs to sleep knowing their code is safe.

The concept was derived from the project's identity: "Silent. Reliable. Nocturnal. Precise." The V is geometric (precise), open-angled (wide field of vision), stroke-based (presence without weight), with a single point of warmth (the vigil's candle in the night).

## Form Principles

- **Geometric, not organic** — Vigil is code, logic, verification. Clean lines, intentional angles.
- **Stroke, not fill** — Vigil observes, doesn't block. Presence without weight.
- **Active negative space** — The space inside the V is where the protected code lives.
- **Single warm accent** — One point of amber against cold precision. The human behind the machine.

## Usage Rules

- Minimum clear space: icon height on all sides
- Minimum size: 24px height for full logo, 16px for icon
- Never stretch, rotate, or add effects
- Over photography: use mono-white variant with sufficient contrast
- The amber accent point must ALWAYS be present — it's the soul of the mark

## What This Logo Is NOT

- Not surveillance — the V is open, not an enclosing eye
- Not aggressive — the angle is wide (~70°), not sharp
- Not decorative — every line has a reason
- Not another generic tech V — the amber point differentiates and tells the story of the vigil

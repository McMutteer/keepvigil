# Vigil — Brand Guide

## The Story: "El Centinela"

A shield with a bold checkmark cutting through it — verification made visible. The shield protects, the checkmark confirms. At the base, an amber diamond — the moment of truth, the score, the candle of the vigil.

The shield is not a wall. It's open at the top — Vigil doesn't block, it observes and verifies. The checkmark is not hidden — it's the hero of the mark, rendered in Vigil Amber against the Steel Watch shield.

## Logo Construction

- **Shield** (`#243a5c`) — Protective enclosure, open at top
- **Checkmark** (`#e8a820`) — Bold amber sweep through the shield = verification
- **Diamond** (`#e8a820`) — Convergence point at base = the verdict

## Usage Rules

- Minimum clear space: icon height on all sides
- Minimum size: 24px for icon, 16px for favicon
- Never stretch, rotate, or add effects
- The amber checkmark must ALWAYS be present — it's the soul of the mark
- Shield color must remain Steel Watch (#243a5c) — not darker, not lighter

## What This Logo Is NOT

- Not a generic shield — the bold amber checkmark differentiates it
- Not surveillance — the shield is open at top, protecting not watching
- Not aggressive — proportions are balanced, amber is warm
- Not decorative — every element has structural purpose

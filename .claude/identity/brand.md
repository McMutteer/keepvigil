# Vigil — Brand Guide

## The Story: "El Centinela"

A shield constructed from a V that opens upward — vigilance embracing what it protects. Inside the shield, a checkmark carved in negative space — verification revealed only on closer inspection. Two sentinel peaks rise at the top, standing watch. At the base where all lines converge, an amber point of light — the moment of truth, the score, the candle of the vigil.

The shield is not a wall. It's open at the top — Vigil doesn't block, it observes and verifies. The checkmark is not drawn on the shield — it's cut out of it. Verification is inherent to the structure, not decorative.

## Form Principles

- **Shield construction** — Two angular wings forming a V, creating a protective enclosure
- **Negative space intelligence** — Checkmark revealed by cutting into the inner panel, not drawn on top
- **Layered depth** — Outer shield (full opacity) + inner core (lighter) = 3D without 3D effects
- **Sentinel peaks** — Two amber-tipped lines rising above the shield = watchtower antennae
- **Single convergence point** — All lines lead to the amber base = the moment of verification

## Usage Rules

- Minimum clear space: icon height on all sides
- Minimum size: 24px height for full logo, 16px for icon
- Never stretch, rotate, or add effects
- The amber accent must ALWAYS be present — it's the soul of the mark
- The checkmark negative space must remain visible — never fill it in
- Over photography: use mono-white variant

## What This Logo Is NOT

- Not a generic shield — the V-opening and negative space checkmark differentiate it
- Not surveillance — the shield is open at top, protecting not watching
- Not aggressive — proportions are balanced, amber is warm
- Not decorative — every element has structural purpose
